## 📌 Brief Overview

This file brings together **5 datasets** from two of the United Nations' primary humanitarian funding mechanisms:

- 🏦 **CERF** — Central Emergency Response Fund
- 🌐 **CBPF** — Country-Based Pooled Funds

Together, they provide a complete picture of global humanitarian aid flows from **2006 to present**.

---

## 🏦 What is CERF?

The **Central Emergency Response Fund (CERF)** is one of the fastest and most effective ways to support rapid humanitarian response for people affected by natural disasters and armed conflicts. Established in **2006 by the United Nations**, CERF receives voluntary contributions from governments, companies, foundations, and individuals, and disburses funds to humanitarian agencies around the world.

## 🌐 What is CBPF?

**Country-Based Pooled Funds (CBPFs)** are UN-managed funds established in countries affected by humanitarian crises. Donors contribute to these pools, and funds are allocated to humanitarian organizations — UN agencies and NGOs — to respond to the most critical needs in-country.

## 🗂️ Dataset Overview

| File | Description | Offical Sources |
|------|-------------|---------|
| `CERF_Donor_contributions.csv` | All donor-level contributions to CERF since 2006 | https://data.humdata.org/dataset/cerf-donor-contributions |
| `CERF_Donor_allocations.csv` | Project-level CERF fund allocations by agency & country | https://data.humdata.org/dataset/cerf-allocations |
| `CERF_Climate_Related_Allocations.csv` | Annual CERF allocations broken down by climate disaster type | https://data.humdata.org/dataset/cerf-climate-related-allocations |
| `CBPF_Contributions.csv` | Donor contributions to individual Country-Based Pooled Funds | https://data.humdata.org/dataset/cbpf-allocations-and-contributions |
| `CBPF_Projects.csv` | Humanitarian projects funded through CBPFs globally | https://data.humdata.org/dataset/cbpf-allocations-and-contributions |
